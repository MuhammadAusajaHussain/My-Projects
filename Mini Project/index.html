Introduction to Photography:

Photography is the art and practice of capturing light with a camera to create images. Whether using a digital camera, a smartphone, or a traditional film camera, photography allows us to document moments, express creativity, and tell stories.

History of Photography:

The history of photography dates back to the early 19th century. The first photograph was taken by Joseph Nicéphore Niépce in 1826 using a process called heliography. Since then, photography has evolved significantly, from the invention of the daguerreotype by Louis Daguerre to the development of modern digital photography.

Types of Photography:

  1. Potrait Photography: Focuses on capturing the likeness, personality, and mood of a person or group.
  2. Landscape Photography: Captures the beauty of nature, including mountains, forests, oceans, and more.
  3. Street Photography: Involves capturing candid moments in public places.
  4. Wildlife Photography: Focuses on capturing images of animals in their natural habitats.
  5. Macro Photography: Involves taking extreme close-up shots of small subjects, such as insects, flowers, or textures.
  6. Event Photography: Includes capturing moments at events like weddings, concerts, and parties.
